About

We are building the bonding layer
for back office work.

The systems work. The components are capable. What’s missing is the substrate that holds them together.

The thesis

Back office work has moved through four eras — paper, systems of record, SaaS sprawl, AI copilots. Each one added more capable components. None of them was a bonding layer. The work moved through systems. The workflow lived in human memory.

Right now humans are the solder. They hold the workflow open through PTO, escalations, retries, reversals, and audits. Your best people are the most fragile component in the circuit. When they break, the work goes silent.

Solden is the substrate built to hold what humans cannot hold reliably: the workflow itself. Persistent, structured, governed, reversible.

Why we start in finance

A thesis must be proven where the cost of failure is highest before it can be claimed where the cost is broader. Finance cannot tolerate workflow ambiguity. A delayed invoice means a missed early-pay discount. A duplicate payment means audit exposure. A stalled close means a delayed earnings report.

Finance has the acute pain. Finance has the budget. Finance is buying.

But the architecture is not finance-specific. The state machine doesn’t care about the domain — it cares about the primitives: long-lived state, deterministic transitions, structured exceptions, reversibility, audit trail. The architecture that runs AP runs procurement, compliance, vendor onboarding, internal audit, and every other back office workflow.

How we build

Deterministic where it matters.
Matching, tolerance, sanctions, budget, three-way logic. The model reads invoices and writes prose. It does not get a vote on routing.
Append-only at the schema layer.
Audit trail enforced by database triggers, not application logic. Every transition carries a hash linked to the prior event’s hash. Strip a row and the chain breaks.
Tools are render targets, not data homes.
Gmail for capture. Slack and Teams for decisions. NetSuite and SAP for the record. The state lives once on the runtime. Surfaces are how operators interact with it.
Sovereign by design.
The workflow record is the operator’s. Not ours. Not the point tool’s. Yours. The runtime is removable; components remain whole if you ever take it out.
